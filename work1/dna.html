DNA

DNA stands for deoxyribonucleic acid and it holds all the instructions necessary for a living organism to function. DNA is found inside the nucleus of a eukaryotic cell, but for a prokaryote, it is found in the cytoplasm. DNA is in the shape of a double helix, with a pentose sugar and a phosphate as the sides of the "ladder" and the nitrogenous bases as the "rungs". There are four nitrogenous bases: adenine(A), thymine(T), guanine(G), and cytosine(C). A and G are purines, while T and C are pyrimidines, and opposites go together. A pairs with T, while C pairs with G. A and T are bonded by two hydrogen bonds, while C and G are bonded by three hydrogen bonds, so C and G have a strong bond than A and T. When undergoing mitosis, DNA replication happens in three steps: initiation, elongation, and termination.
